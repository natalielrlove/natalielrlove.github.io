We study how biodiversity shapes ecosystem function and services in cities, and what urban green spaces can offer for plant conservation. Our work spans local field experiments, city-scale remote sensing, and global tree inventory datasets — united by a commitment to understanding and improving urban landscapes for both people and plants.

**We are based at the [Chicago Botanic Garden](https://www.chicagobotanic.org) and [Northwestern University](https://www.northwestern.edu).**

---

### Research Areas

- **Biodiversity & Ecosystem Function** — How does plant diversity affect urban cooling, microclimate, and ecosystem services?
- **Urban Forests as Conservation Tools** — What role can cities play in conserving threatened tree species and genetic diversity?
- **Environmental Equity** — Is vegetation quality, not just quantity, inequitably distributed across communities?
